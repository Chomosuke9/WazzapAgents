# Tips & Best Practices

## For Group Admins

1. **Always test in a testing group first** before enabling moderation in a real group
2. **Start with a low permission level** (0 or 1), then increase once you're confident
3. **Create prompts with clear boundaries** — a bot that doesn't know its limits can overreact
4. **Use `/reset` after making major prompt changes** so the bot isn't confused by old context
5. **Actively monitor the group** especially during early moderation use

## For Writing Moderation Prompts

- **Explicitly** state the conditions for when to kick vs just warn
- Provide a **warning system** before kicking (e.g., 3-5 warnings first)
- Clearly define what "toxic" or "spam" means by your group's standards
- Avoid ambiguous instructions like "delete all bad messages" — the bot might misinterpret

## For Regular Users

- The bot responds best when you **mention or reply** directly to the bot
- Send **one topic per message** for best results
- If the bot gives an irrelevant answer, try **rephrasing** your question more specifically
- Use `/reset` if the conversation has become too chaotic
